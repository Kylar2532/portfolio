Alex graduated from the University of Minnesota -- Twin Cities in December 2023 with a Bachelor's of Arts in Computer Science and minor in Music. He was offered a spot in the University's Computer Science Master's program, which he is thrilled to accept and is eagerly awaiting the start of.

When it comes to programming, Alex has two primary loves: graphics (including animation) and functional programming. He is planning on specializing in graphics during his Master's program, but as of the current moment is undecided which niche he will focus on. In an ideal world, he would be able to create graphics and animations (suffice to say, one of his current not-yet-ready-for-public-premier projects is to create complete graphics and animation libraries for functional langauges such as OCaml and F#).

Over the past few years, Alex has had the joy of being an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant at the University of Minnesota for the CS course Advanced Programming Principles; the functional programming course required for any Computer Science degree. During this time, Alex was frequently questioned be students (rather increduously) why he loves OCaml so much, and told that the only other person students had met whose genuine passion for the language matched his was the course professor.

Outside of the academic realm, Alex is an avid rock climber and can often be found throwing himself up the walls at Minneapolis Bouldering Project with his friends. He also enjoys tabletop games, reading books (especially fantasy), baking, and spending time with his partner and their cats (and encouraging his partner's crochet hobby by regularly sending him fun new patterns).
